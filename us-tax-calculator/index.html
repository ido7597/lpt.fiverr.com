Frequently asked questions

How accurate are online tax calculators for the 2024 tax season?

Tax calculators provide estimates based on the information you input. Their accuracy depends on the completeness and correctness of the data you provide. Please note that as these are AI-driven estimates, they may contain inaccuracies. While they're excellent tools for planning, for a more precise calculation that accounts for your specific circumstances and the latest tax law changes for 2024, consulting with one of our tax professionals is recommended. They can help ensure you're taking advantage of all available deductions and credits.

Is this tax calculator and service only for US taxpayers?

Yes, our tax calculator and expert services are specifically designed for US federal and state tax regulations. The calculations, deductions, credits, and tax brackets are based on US tax law for the 2024 tax year. We currently do not support tax calculations or consultations for other countries' tax systems. If you need assistance with international tax matters or non-US tax returns, we recommend seeking experts who specialize in the specific country's tax regulations. Our platform can help connect US taxpayers with experts who have experience in international tax implications for US citizens.

What data sources and logic is the tax calculator based on?

Our tax calculator is based on official IRS tax tables, rates, and guidelines for the 2024 tax year as published in IRS Revenue Procedure 2023-34 and related IRS publications. The standard deduction amounts, tax brackets, credit eligibility rules, and other calculations follow the official IRS guidelines. The calculator also incorporates data from the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act (TCJA) provisions that remain in effect for 2024. While we strive for accuracy, this calculator provides estimates only and should not be considered official tax or legal advice. Tax laws are subject to change, and individual circumstances may require professional consultation. We recommend verifying all calculations with an official IRS publication or a qualified tax professional.
