Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about tax calculators and finding the right tax expert for the 2024 tax season. If you can't find what you're looking for, you can always contact our support team.

What types of tax calculators are available for the 2024 tax season?

Several types of tax calculators are available to help you with your 2024 taxes, including tools for estimating your tax refund, calculating your tax bracket, determining W-4 withholding, estimating self-employment tax, and calculating capital gains for cryptocurrency. Our tax experts can help you choose the right calculator for your specific situation and guide you through the process to ensure accurate results.

How accurate are online tax calculators for the 2024 tax season?

Tax calculators provide estimates based on the information you input. Their accuracy depends on the completeness and correctness of the data you provide. While they're excellent tools for planning, for a more precise calculation that accounts for your specific circumstances and the latest tax law changes for 2024, consulting with one of our tax professionals is recommended. They can help ensure you're taking advantage of all available deductions and credits.

What are the key tax changes I should know about for the 2024 tax season?

For 2024, several important changes may affect your taxes: The standard deduction has increased to $14,600 for single filers and $29,200 for married couples filing jointly. IRA contribution limits are now $7,000 ($8,000 if you're 50+), and 401(k) limits are $23,000 ($30,500 if you're 50+). The IRS Direct File system is now a permanent option for eligible taxpayers. Our tax experts stay current with all tax law changes to help maximize your refund and ensure compliance.

How do I find a qualified tax expert for the 2024 tax season?

Finding the right tax expert is crucial for maximizing your refund and ensuring compliance. Look for professionals with credentials like a Preparer Tax Identification Number (PTIN), Enrolled Agent (EA) status, or Certified Public Accountant (CPA) certification. Our platform makes it easy to find verified tax experts with these qualifications. You can filter by expertise, review ratings and client feedback, and choose someone who specializes in your specific tax situation.

What questions should I ask a potential tax expert before hiring them?

When hiring a tax expert, ask about their experience with situations similar to yours, their qualifications and credentials, their fee structure, and whether they'll sign your return and include their PTIN. Ensure they can clearly explain tax concepts and answer your questions about the 2024 tax changes. Also confirm they'll handle your information securely and confidentially. Our platform makes it easy to communicate with potential tax experts before making your decision.

What is IRS Direct File and should I use it for my 2024 taxes?

IRS Direct File is a free service that allows eligible taxpayers to file their federal taxes directly with the IRS online. It's now a permanent option starting with the 2024 tax season. Eligibility depends on living in a participating state and having certain types of income and deductions. While Direct File is convenient for simple returns, our tax experts can help determine if it's right for you or if you'd benefit from professional assistance to maximize deductions and credits that might be missed with self-filing.

How can a tax expert help me maximize my refund for the 2024 tax season?

Tax experts can identify deductions and credits you might overlook, such as the increased standard deduction, retirement contribution benefits, and special credits for 2024. They stay current with tax law changes, like the expanded Premium Tax Credit and new rules for retirement plan emergency withdrawals. Additionally, they can provide personalized tax planning strategies based on your specific financial situation, potentially saving you significantly more than their fee costs. Our platform connects you with experts who specialize in maximizing refunds.
